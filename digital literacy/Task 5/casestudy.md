#  Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying is a form of online harassment where individuals are targeted through social media, messaging platforms, or other digital channels. It often involves spreading rumors, posting hurtful comments, sharing private information, or sending threatening messages.

In a realistic scenario, a college student posts a photo on social media, and a group of classmates begins leaving negative and insulting comments. Over time, the bullying escalates into creating fake accounts to mock the student and sharing edited images to embarrass them. The victim may feel isolated, anxious, and afraid to participate online or even attend college.

Cyberbullying usually targets teenagers and young adults who are active on social media platforms. The anonymity of the internet makes it easier for offenders to act without immediate consequences.

The impact of cyberbullying can be severe, including emotional distress, loss of confidence, academic decline, and mental health issues. In extreme cases, it can lead to depression or self-harm. This highlights the importance of respectful online behavior and awareness about digital responsibility.
